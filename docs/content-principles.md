This document is the source of truth for Learn / Journeys / Tools writing style, pharmacopoeial content handling, and practical advice expression.

BioassayCraft content should help practitioners understand scientific reasoning and apply it responsibly in bioassay work.

It should not sound like a regulatory archive, software manual, or marketing site.

BioassayCraft does not currently have a traditional Articles section. Text-led content should support a specific learning, journey, tool, or case-study purpose rather than become a generic blog or article stream.

## Core Writing Flow

Prefer this order:

```text
Conclusion
  -> Basis
  -> Scope
  -> Practice reminder
```

Users should quickly understand the point, then see why it is true, where it applies, and what to remember in practice.

## Module Writing

### Learn

Learn explains pharmacopoeial and statistical ideas.

Write Learn content to answer:

```text
What does the rule mean?
Why is the calculation structured this way?
Where do practitioners commonly misunderstand it?
```

Avoid long article structure. Prefer short concept blocks, case data, formulas, interactive controls, visual explanation, and mentor notes.

### Journeys

Journeys translate rules into project workflows.

Write Journey content to answer:

```text
What decision is being made now?
What information does the practitioner need?
What does this imply for the next step?
```

A Journey should help users move through a real method development, validation, data analysis, or system suitability path.

### Tools

Tools calculate, simulate, or explore a focused question.

Write Tool content to answer:

```text
What scientific question does this tool answer?
What assumptions are active?
What does the result mean?
What should the user not over-interpret?
```

Tools should remain direct. Do not over-teach, but do not leave users with unexplained numbers.

## Pharmacopoeial Content

Rules:

- Do not copy long pharmacopoeial text.
- Reference chapter numbers, concepts, formula logic, and case structure when needed.
- Explain in BioassayCraft's own language.
- Use interaction, visualization, and adjustable parameters to create original learning value.
- Distinguish regulatory requirements, statistical derivation, and practical advice.
- Distinguish mandatory requirements from recommended practice.
- Distinguish early development, IND, BLA / commercial, and lifecycle contexts.
- Mark uncertain or region-dependent content clearly.

Use wording such as:

```text
Confirm against the current project stage and submission region.
```

when the answer depends on regulatory context.

## Mentor Notes

Mentor Notes should explain:

- Why the idea matters.
- When it becomes important.
- What is commonly misunderstood.
- How the result affects practical judgement.

They should not be tooltips, definitions, or regulatory paraphrases.

Good Mentor Notes reduce anxiety by revealing the reasoning path.

## Practice Advice

Practice advice should be careful and bounded.

Avoid:

- Over-confident universal recommendations.
- Claims that a tool replaces statistical review.
- Language that sounds like formal regulatory approval.
- Hiding uncertainty behind confident automation.

Prefer:

- Short, objective interpretation.
- Explicit assumptions.
- Practical implications.
- Clear scope of use.

## Content Tone

The tone should be:

- Calm.
- Scientific.
- Precise.
- Helpful.
- Practice-aware.

It should feel like a carefully annotated scientific notebook from someone who understands real bioassay work.
