# BioassayCraft Manifesto

BioassayCraft is an interactive scientific learning platform for bioassay practitioners.

It is not only a bioassay version of Seeing Theory. It should go further by connecting theory, regulation, visualization, simulation, calculation, and real-world assay practice.

This manifesto is the highest-level guide for BioassayCraft product, design, engineering, and content decisions.

## Core Purpose

BioassayCraft exists to bridge the gap between regulatory and scientific theory and practical bioassay work.

Many pharmacopoeial chapters, regulatory guidelines, and statistical theories are scientifically sound but difficult to apply directly in real laboratory and CMC decision-making contexts. BioassayCraft should translate that gap into interactive learning journeys, practical tools, visual explanations, and carefully reasoned case-based guidance.

## Primary Audience

Prioritize:

1. Bioassay scientists and analytical development practitioners.
2. CMC analytical scientists who need to understand bioassay thinking.
3. New practitioners entering the field.
4. Technical leaders or managers who need clearer frameworks for method decisions.

Do not optimize primarily for general audiences, academic abstraction, or regulatory encyclopedic completeness.

## Desired Identity

BioassayCraft should be remembered as:

- A platform for bioassay thinking.
- A practical bridge between theory and laboratory reality.
- An interactive learning environment for method development, validation, model reasoning, and scientific decision-making.
- The bioassay equivalent of Seeing Theory, but more practice-oriented and journey-based.

It should not be remembered merely as a calculator collection.

## Emotional Standard

BioassayCraft should not only be useful. It should make users feel that the product understands their real work.

Users should feel:

- The theory becomes clearer.
- The practical decision is less lonely.
- Hidden assumptions are made visible.
- The tool respects their intelligence.
- The platform is calm, careful, and built by someone who has faced similar problems.

Avoid cold, mechanical, or purely utilitarian experiences.

## Visual Temperament

BioassayCraft should not feel cold, mechanical, or merely technical.

Its visual temperament should feel like a carefully edited scientific notebook made by an East Asian designer: calm, restrained, warm, precise, and quietly cared for.

The site should communicate not only scientific rigor, but also intellectual care.

Users should feel that:

- The author understands where real practitioners get confused.
- Complexity is unfolded gradually.
- Explanations reduce anxiety rather than create pressure.
- Interaction feels guided rather than rushed.
- The interface respects their intelligence.
- The product was built by someone who has faced similar practical problems.

The goal is not decoration. The goal is care through clarity.

## Product Form

BioassayCraft should grow through learning journeys rather than isolated utilities.

Preferred structure:

```text
Journey
  -> Concept
  -> Interactive explanation
  -> Simulation / Explorer
  -> Calculator / Practical tool
  -> Interpretation
  -> Case or practice note
  -> Scientific reference
```

Examples of future journeys:

- Method Development Journey
- Bioassay Validation Journey
- ANOVA and Model Comparison Journey
- AQbD Journey
- Relative Potency Journey
- System Suitability Journey
- OOS and Risk Reasoning Journey

Individual tools may exist, but they should ideally belong to a larger scientific learning journey.

## AI Role

AI may be used as an assistant, reviewer, or implementation collaborator.

AI should not be treated as the scientific decision maker.

AI can help with:

- Explanation.
- Code generation.
- Content drafting.
- Review.
- Formatting.
- Interaction design.
- Implementation support.

AI must not silently decide or modify:

- Scientific formulas.
- Regulatory interpretation.
- Statistical models.
- Validation logic.
- Acceptance criteria.
- Scientific conclusions.

When uncertainty exists, the product should surface uncertainty rather than hide it behind confident automation.

## Business Principle

Core website content should remain free.

Free content builds trust, professional credibility, and long-term community value.

Revenue, if developed, should come from adjacent professional services such as:

- Consulting.
- Training.
- Project review.
- Workshop design.
- Method development or validation strategy support.
- Enterprise-specific advisory work.

Do not compromise the open learning value of the website for short-term monetization.

## Decision Standard

Every major product, content, design, or engineering decision should pass this test:

```text
Does this help bioassay practitioners understand the science more clearly,
apply it more responsibly in practice,
and make better method decisions?
```

If not, it should not be prioritized.

## Non-Goals

BioassayCraft should not become:

- A generic calculator collection.
- A regulatory document archive.
- A marketing-heavy SaaS site.
- A generic AI chatbot.
- A bioinformatics platform.
- A broad biopharma encyclopedia.
- A visually impressive but scientifically shallow demo site.

## Highest Principle

BioassayCraft should help people move from "I know the rule" to "I understand the reasoning and can apply it in practice."

That is the product's highest goal.
