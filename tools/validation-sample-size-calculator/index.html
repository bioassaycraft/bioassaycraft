Validation Sample Size Calculator

This page helps you understand why validation sample size depends on the distance between expected variability and the acceptance criterion.

Scientific question

How many independent measurements are needed before a validation study has enough chance to demonstrate the planned accuracy or precision criterion?

Scientific idea

Sample size grows when the expected method behavior sits close to the acceptance margin. The calculator makes that pressure visible before reducing it to a single n.

Mentor note

Minimum n is not just a planning number. It is a statement about confidence, power, expected variability, and how much scientific margin the validation design has left.

Interaction

Use the controls to test whether the design is driven by accuracy pressure, precision pressure, confidence, or power.

Scope of use

This calculator is organized around USP <1033> and ChP 9401 terminology for learning and validation design support. It keeps accuracy-driven and precision-driven reasoning visible, but it does not replace formal statistical review.
