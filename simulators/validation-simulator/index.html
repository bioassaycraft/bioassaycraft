Scientific idea

Validation is not a single pass/fail table. It is a chain of reasoning from ATP, through design and data, to the reliability of the reportable result.

Mentor note

Follow the same data as it changes role: first as a planned matrix, then as observed evidence, then as procedure performance, method variance, and final decision support.

Potency Level Recommendation

Potency levels should test the method beyond the routine decision range while staying interpretable on the log scale.

Confirmed potency levels

USP <1033> recommends extending the dilutional linearity study beyond release specification limits, with ICH Q2(R2) suggesting 80% of the lower limit and 120% of the upper limit. The values below are suggested rounded, log-scale balanced validation levels.

Recommended validation levels
0.50, 0.71, 1.00, 1.41, 2.00

These levels extend beyond 80% of the lower limit and 120% of the upper limit, include the target potency of 1.00, and are approximately symmetric on the log scale.
